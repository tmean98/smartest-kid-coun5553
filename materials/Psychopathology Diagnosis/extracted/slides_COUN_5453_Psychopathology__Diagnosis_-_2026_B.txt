Course Description:
This course focuses on the diagnosis process, differential diagnosis, and treatment of the
psychopathologies and how those pathologies differ in various cultures. Emphasis is placed on
the use of the current Diagnostic and Statistical Manual of Mental Disorders (DSM).
Course Objectives:
• Apply processes of differential diagnosis using the DSM.
• Evaluate cultural and spiritual differences in definitions of psychopathology.
• Integrate understanding of psychopathology with counseling theory and practice.

Final Exam- Diagnostic Case Vignette: There is one written exam in this course. The exam
will require students to provide an accurate diagnosis based on information gathered from a
vignette provided by the professor. In the response, students will describe symptoms that meet
diagnostic criteria, provide an accurate diagnosis and thorough differential diagnosis using all the
steps of differential diagnosis, and a basic treatment plan. A rubric for the exam is provided at
the end of the syllabus.
THIS IS A KEY ASSESSMENT: This exam serves as one of the key assessments for the
program and is used to evaluate entry level diagnostic skills. As such, students must pass the
exam to receive a passing grade in the class. Given the significance of the exam, students who do
not pass the exam will be offered the opportunity to engage in remediation of skills and retake
the exam.
C) Response, Research, or Other Written Assignments
Case Vignettes
Students select a mental health disorder from the readings covered in each class session and
write a detailed vignette that includes the following:
• A brief description of the client including mental status, relevant mental health, medical,
personal history and demographic information.
• A detailed description of symptoms relevant to mental health disorder selected. The
description of symptoms should allow classmates to confirm or rule out a diagnosis
• A properly formatted DSM diagnosis for the case.
Vignettes are due before class and will be posted to discovery.
Additionally, students will need to bring a copy of the vignette to share in class. The copy shared
in class should not include the DSM diagnosis as it will be used as a part of an in-class exercise
which will require students to evaluate the vignette and provide an accurate diagnosis.

Clinical Interview Video- Due March 25
Students will work with a partner to take turns recording a clinical interview role play. For this
project, each student will take the role of the clinician and the client.
As the clinician, the student must conduct a brief, 10-minute diagnostic interview using the skills
covered in the course. The aim of the assignment is to practice asking open and closed questions
to explore the client’s experience with symptoms that may help inform a diagnosis. NOTE- a
diagnosis is not required as the time frame for the interview is limited. Rather, the exercise is the
demonstration of skills related to the clinical interview.
As the client, the student will role play one of the characters created from the case vignettes. The
student should be prepared to answer clinical interview questions that help lead to greater
understanding of symptoms and a possible diagnosis. Therefore, the student should be familiar
with the client’s history and history of the illness (IE, onset, duration and frequency of
symptoms, severity, any related specifiers, etc.).
The video may be recorded via Microsoft Stream or Teams. Students will save the video to their
NU One Drive and share the link with the professor by submitting a document with the link to
Discovery.
