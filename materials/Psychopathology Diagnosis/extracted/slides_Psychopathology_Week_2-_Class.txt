What about reading the DSM?
Depressive Disorders
Sad, irritable, and cognitive changes
Addition of Disruptive Mood Dysregulation Disorder
Avoid over diagnosis of Bipolar in children
Differs from grief, “normal” sadness and mood changes
(APA, 2022)
Disruptive Mood Dysregulation	
Persistent and frequent irritability and aggression
Temper outbursts and chronic irritability
Aggression is more that what would be considered appropriate to situation
Onset before age 10
Must rule out development disorders
Differs from bipolar based on lack of shift from depression and irritable mood
DMD does not typical shift to bipolar, but to depression and anxiety
(APA, 2022)
Major Depressive Disorder	
Low mood and loss of interest in ALL activities
Disturbances 
sleep, appetite, psychomotor activity
Feelings of guilt, hopelessness, suicidal ideation
Fatigue and decreased energy, concertation issues
Agitation and Irritability
Specifiers
(APA, 2022)
Persistent and Premenstrual Dysphoric Disorders
Persistent Depressive Disorder
May occur after MD episode and MD symptoms may continue
2-year period of symptoms
Less than 2 months without symptoms
Premenstrual Dysphoric Disorder
Mood changes, anxiety and irritability that are beyond typical experience
Start before Pre-menstrual Phase and subside within a week following the start of menses
Cause clinically significant distress
(APA, 2022)
Anxiety Related Disorders
Fear vs. Anxiety
Imminent vs. Future Orientation
Disorders differentiated by type of situation or fear object
Separation Anxiety

Generalized Anxiety
Different from developmentally appropriate worry
 persistent, excessive- determination made by clinician
APA, 2022
Separation Anxiety
Distress about separation from attachment figure
 In children worry about harm to care giver- harm to self- getting lost kidnapped– in adults discomfort traveling alone, nightmares , excessive worry about family or other loved ones
Onset as early as preschool, Typically resolves with age–
Increased risk with life stress and trauma
Key words are persistent and excessive--- 
APA, 2022
Selective Mutism
Failure to speak in specific social situations
Not absence of ability to speak- refusal/failure to speak
Tendencies of shyness but beyond typical expressions of shyness
Use of nonverbal communication
Interferes with work, school or social engagement
Experienced for at least 1 month
Not related to lack on knowledge or another communication disorder
APA, 2022
Specific Phobia		
Marked Fear or Anxiety of Situations or Objects
Different from “normal” fears
Out of proportion with expected fear response
A response every time stimulus is encountered
Experiences may range from worry to panic
Must take action to avoid objects or situations
Obvious or subtle actions
APA, 2022
Social Anxiety	
Fear or worry about being judged/evaluated by others
Children must include fear related to peers
Humiliation, embarrassment, and/or rejection, afraid others will see anxiety
Avoid fear situations- eating, drinking, speaking etc.
Almost always experience fear or worry
Vary in degree and duration
Out of proportion and beyond normative shyness
On set typical in adolescence
More intense in adolescents but broader in adulthood
APA, 2022
Panic Disorder
Repeated sudden panic attacks
Quick onset
Fear of having more attacks
Avoidant behavior
Worry that attacks are related to other health concerns, fear other will notice panic symptoms, fear of losing control
Unexpected vs expected
Clinician evaluates and determines
Frequency and intensity vary
Onset typical in early 20’s
APA, 2022
Generalized Anxiety Disorder
Excessive worry/anxiety about multiple areas
Difficulty controlling worry
Everyday type actives/events
Beyond typical life concerns
Pervasive and long duration
Symptoms “wax and wane”
