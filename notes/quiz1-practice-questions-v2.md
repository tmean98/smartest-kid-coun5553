# ANSWER KEY

## Chapter 1
1. **B** — Legal standards are the minimum behavior society will tolerate; ethical standards are the minimum counselors will tolerate from each other; best practice is the ideal. (Class 1 slides p. 47)
2. **B** — Morality refers to personal beliefs and how they affect conduct, whereas ethics refers to professional behaviors. (Ch. 1, p. 3; textbook summary p. 26)
3. **C** — The eight-step model: (1) Identify the problem, (2) Involve client, (3) Review ethics codes, (4) Consider principles and virtues, (5) Tune into feelings, (6) **Consult with colleagues or experts**, (7) Consider context, (8) Make a decision. (Class 1 slides pp. 69-70; Class 2 slides pp. 50-51)
4. **C** — Absolutism holds that moral principles are fixed and must be followed regardless of the outcome or context. This differs from deontology (rule-based, Kant) but shares the idea that certain actions are right or wrong in themselves. (Ch. 1, p. 10)

## Chapter 2
5. **C** — The four beliefs are: (1) wellness model, (2) developmental perspective, (3) prevention and early intervention, and (4) empowerment of clients. The medical model is NOT one of them — counseling explicitly distinguishes itself from the medical model. (Ch. 2, pp. 30-34; Class 1 slides pp. 29-32)
6. **C** — Virginia passed the first counselor licensure law in 1976. (Ch. 2, p. 45; Class 1 slides p. 39)
7. **C** — NBCC was created in 1982 as the national agency to certify counselors. (Ch. 2, p. 39)
8. **B** — ACA Code C.4.d. states counselors should not imply doctoral-level competence in counseling when their doctorate is not in counseling or a closely related field. (Ch. 2, pp. 41-42)

## Chapter 3
9. **B** — ADDRESSING: Age, Developmental and acquired Disability, Religion/spirituality, Ethnicity, Socioeconomic status, Sexual orientation, Indigenous status, Nationality, Gender. (Class 2 slides p. 15; Hays, 2002)
10. **B** — Marcia's theory states that identities defined externally (by others) rather than internally are foreclosures. (Class 2 slides p. 34)
11. **B** — Social justice = promoting access for all people to mental health services, especially marginalized populations. Advocacy = commitment to social justice through ACTION to promote change. (Class 2 slides p. 21)
12. **B** — "Double vision" (Eriksson & Abernethy, 2014, drawing on Volf) means seeing through another's perspective empathically without abandoning one's own views — requiring a high level of differentiation. (Class 2 slides p. 49)

## Chapter 4
13. **B** — Countertransference is when the counselor's own feelings, attitudes, and unresolved issues become entangled in the therapeutic relationship. Transference is when the *client* projects past feelings onto the counselor. (Class 3 slides pp. 8-11)
14. **C** — Informed consent must include the role of technology and a backup plan if tech fails (item 5 on the 10-element list). It is both a legal AND ethical obligation, and it is an ongoing process — not a one-time event. (Class 2 slides p. 70; Class 3 slides p. 26)
15. **B** — ACA C.7.a. requires that specialized techniques be grounded in empirical research. (Class 2 slides p. 60; Class 3 slides p. 15)
16. **C** — Fiduciary means the counselor acts in the client's benefit. "Clients are not there for your benefit, you are there for them." (Class 2 slides p. 57; Class 3 slides p. 7)

## Chapter 5
17. **B** — Privacy is the broadest term (individual's right to decide what is shared). Confidentiality is an explicit promise between counselor and client. Privileged communication is a legal protection in court proceedings. (Class 3 slides p. 37)
18. **B** — Privilege belongs to the client, not the counselor. The client asserts or waives the privilege. If the client cannot be located, the counselor should assert it on their behalf. (Class 3 slides pp. 69, 71)
19. **B** — The Bradley case established the duty not to negligently release a dangerous client. (Class 3 slides p. 52)
20. **B** — Under RCW 71.34.650, parents may seek outpatient mental health services for adolescents 13+ for up to 12 sessions in a 3-month period without the adolescent's consent. (Class 3 slides p. 45)

## Chapter 6
21. **B** — ACA B.6.e. states that competent clients should have reasonable access to their counseling records. (Class 4 slides p. 4)
22. **C** — HIPAA does NOT allow automatic sharing with family members without the client's knowledge. However, if clients are informed and given an opportunity to orally object, counselors may share information with family members assisting in the client's care. (Class 4 slides pp. 15-16)
23. **B** — SOAP: Subjective (client reported), Objective (counselor observed), Assessment (making sense of S and O), Plan (action steps). (Class 4 slides pp. 6-7)

## Chapter 7
24. **C** — Nonmaleficence ("do no harm") is the most salient moral principle related to competence. Practicing outside one's competence risks harming the client. (Class 4 slides p. 21; Ch. 7, p. 161)
25. **B** — Vicarious traumatization is listed alongside environmental factors and preexisting conditions as risk factors for counselor impairment. (Class 4 slides p. 33; Ch. 7, pp. 173-176)
26. **B** — WA LMHC requires passing the NCE/NCMHCE AND 3,000 hours (1,200 direct, 100 supervision) or 3 years of full-time counseling work. (Class 4 slides p. 25)
27. **B** — ACA C.2.g and F.5.b state that impaired counselors must limit, suspend, or terminate their professional responsibilities and seek assistance. (Class 4 slides p. 36)

## Chapter 8
28. **C** — The next leading cause of malpractice lawsuits (after sexual relationships) involves situations where clients attempt or complete suicide. (Class 4 slides p. 53; Ch. 8, p. 199)
29. **True** — Courts can hold counselors accountable for both overreacting (e.g., unnecessary involuntary commitment) and underreacting (e.g., failing to take protective steps). (Class 4 slides p. 58; Ch. 8)
30. **B** — The recommended first step is to respond directly to the person — calm them down and listen to their concerns. Do NOT admit wrongdoing. (Class 4 slides p. 65)
